Study WorkFlow

NDVI

NDVI = (NIR-Red)/(NIR+Red). Since the heather vegetation absorbs EM radiation in visible portion of the spectrum mainly in Red and blue, and reflect in Green and NIR, NDVI uses NIR which is strongly reflected and Red which is strongly absorbed. NDVI was employed to visually differentiate the spectral significance in vegetation cover across the study region. It can be analysed from an image that as the level change from 0 to 1 the greener the vegetation and opposite is true where build up areas are around about 0 and water such as sea has greater negative index which indicate the absence of vegetation; however, some water may indicate the concentration of NDVI, and this is caused by the presence of algae in the water.

Index Calculation

NDWI= (NIR-SWIR)/(NIR+SWIR). NDWI is used to enhance water bodies in most cases. Water has low reflectance and decreases from visible portion of spectrum. NDWI and MNDWI are widely used to highlight water.

View
Classification

MNDWI= (Green-SWIR)/(Green+SWIR). There is differentiated phenomenon between NDWI and MNDWI where MNDWI strongly highlight water than NDWI. NDWI produces poor quality results hence using MNDWI can benefit the analysis.

Classified Image

Figure show the classified images from 1985, 1995, 2015 and 2021 using Naïve Bayes ML Algorithm in GEE. It can be visual seen that there is a change of soil, cultivation, urban, forest and agriculture due to development. The rate of change of cultivation changes the appearance of agricultural areas including cultivation or deforestation. Around the port of Richards’ bay we can observed the sudden development and build up area from 1985 to 2021, this is due to industrial development and migration. There is a change in the soil (sea sand) which is seemed to be influence by forest and agricultural areas.

Overal Accuracy:

1985 Classification = 79%, 1995 Classification = 76%, 2015 Classification = 68%, 2021 Classification = 74%
